Mission & History

Our Mission:

The mission of Arlington Life Shelter is to promote self-sufficiency by providing shelter, employment programs and transitional services for North Texans impacted by homelessness.

Our Vision:

Arlington Life Shelter envisions a community where all individuals, families, and children affected by homelessness have access to the services and support necessary to restore ability and achieve personalized success.

Agency Overview:

Over 30,000 homeless men, women, and children have sought refuge at the Arlington Life Shelter over the past 37 years. On a very cold night in 1987, volunteers opened the doors of First Presbyterian Church of Arlington, welcoming those with no place to call home. During the first three years, the Shelter was operated entirely by volunteers. It moved to its current location in 1988 and was renovated in 1993, increasing the capacity for clients from 43 to 87. In summer 2019, construction began on the shelter expansion temporarily reducing the number of beds to 45.

The facility became operational on June 4, 2020 with increased space, a computer room, shared lounges, seven family bedrooms, and an expanded children’s programming space called Carol's Clubhouse. In the beginning, most guests were unemployed men, often challenged by alcohol or mental illness. Today, women and children make up over half the shelter population. Changing demographics have seen the shelter expand from providing cold weather shelter to the comprehensive wrap-around services offered today.

Our Residents:

Homeless individuals and families arrive from throughout the Metroplex for a number of reasons. The lack of affordable housing, underemployment and unemployment, eviction, medical debt, and addiction are most often identified as the catalyst(s) for homelessness. Over half of the parents entering the facility are employed, but not earning enough to pay for housing and family necessities.

A New Day. A New Home. A New Life.
